**Models come to your data — your data never leaves your infrastructure.** You run a tracebloc *client* on your own hardware. Contributors submit models that are scanned and run in isolation against your data, on your machines. Only metrics and trained weights ever leave.

Raw data stays inside the box. The client opens an **outbound-only** connection — nothing reaches in to pull your data out.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deploy a client on your infrastructure">
    One client per machine — a laptop, an on-prem server, or a cloud cluster. It runs a self-contained Kubernetes environment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stage your datasets">
    You ingest datasets locally. Metadata syncs to the platform so contributors can see what's available — **the raw data never moves.**
  </Step>
  <Step title="A contributor submits a model">
    Submitted models are **scanned for vulnerabilities** before anything runs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The model trains against your data — on your hardware">
    Training runs in an **isolated container** with restricted network and system access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Only results leave">
    Evaluation metrics and trained weights flow back out over TLS. Your data does not.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What stays, what leaves

| Stays on your infrastructure | Leaves (outbound, TLS) | Enforced by |
|---|---|---|
| Raw training data | Dataset *metadata* (schema, counts) | Per-job container isolation |
| Datasets at rest | Evaluation *metrics* | NetworkPolicy — no data egress from training pods |
| Training compute | Trained model *weights* | Vulnerability scan before any model runs |
| | | TLS on all client ↔ platform traffic |

<Note>
**The mental model:** `1 machine = 1 client = 1 workspace = n datasets`. One deployment per machine; as many datasets inside it as you like.
</Note>

## Vocabulary

| Term | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Client** | The tracebloc agent running on your infrastructure |
| **Workspace** | Your client's identity on one machine (its Kubernetes namespace) |
| **Dataset** | Data you've staged locally for use cases |
| **Use case** | A task contributors build models for, against your datasets |
| **Contributor** | An external data scientist who submits models — and never sees your raw data |

## What it touches

The installer changes only **Docker** and **`~/.tracebloc`** on your host — no system-wide changes. Uninstalling is a single command, and your data is yours throughout.
