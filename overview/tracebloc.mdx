## What is tracebloc?

tracebloc is a **collaborative AI workspace** you deploy on your own infrastructure. Anyone you invite — researchers, partners, vendors, startups — can train, fine-tune, and benchmark models on your data. Your data never moves. Compliance is solved by architecture.

**Who is it for?** Anyone who asks "which model works best on MY data?" and wants external input without the nightmare of NDAs, data-sharing agreements, and security reviews.

**What makes it different?**

- **Your infrastructure:** runs on your Mac, Linux box, GPU server, or any Kubernetes cluster
- **Invite anyone:** whitelist contributors by email. They see EDA and metadata. Never raw data.
- **One leaderboard:** every submission benchmarked under identical conditions. Ship the winner.
- **Compliance by architecture:** data never moves. Sign off once on the architecture, not once per partner.

**What do you need?** Docker, 4\+ CPU cores, 8 GB RAM, 20 GB disk, and 10 minutes.

## How It Works

1. **Deploy on your hardware** — one script. Your Mac, a Linux workstation, an NVIDIA GPU box, or any Kubernetes cluster.
2. **Define a use case** — select datasets, set evaluation metrics. Use a template or build your own.
3. **Invite anyone** — whitelist contributors by email. Assign compute budgets. They never see raw data.
4. **Build together** — contributors submit models and train inside your environment. PyTorch, TensorFlow, custom containers.
5. **Compare and decide** — every submission benchmarked under identical conditions. One leaderboard. Ship the winner.

## Security

tracebloc enforces strict separation of code and data at the infrastructure level.

- **Raw data never leaves your infrastructure.** Training runs execute locally. Only metadata and aggregated metrics are shared.
- **Model artifacts remain under the workspace owner's control** and are never shared without explicit mutual consent.
- **All communication is TLS-encrypted.** The tracebloc client connects to the backend over secure channels (port 443).
- **Contributors interact only with metadata and training metrics.** No access to raw data at any point.

For architecture details and security configuration — see the [Environment Setup](/environment-setup/configuration) docs.

## Quick Setup

Get from zero to a running workspace in under 10 minutes.

### Prerequisites

- **Docker** — installed automatically if missing
- **Hardware** — 4\+ CPU cores, 8\+ GB RAM, 20\+ GB free disk
- **Network** — outbound access to `*.docker.io`, `*.tracebloc.io`

### 1. Create an Account

Sign up at [ai.tracebloc.io](https://ai.tracebloc.io). Free to get started.

### 2. Register a Node

Open the [client page](https://ai.tracebloc.io/clients) and click **"\+"**. Fill in a name, location, and password. Note your **Client ID** and **password** — you need both in the next step.

<Tip>
  Provisioning takes a few seconds. Refresh the page if the status doesn't update.
</Tip>

### 3. Deploy

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="macOS / Linux">
    ```bash
    bash <(curl -fsSL https://tracebloc.io/install.sh)
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Windows">
    Open **PowerShell as Administrator**:

    ```powershell
    irm https://tracebloc.io/install.ps1 | iex
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

The installer prompts for your workspace name, Client ID, and password. It handles Docker, Kubernetes, and the tracebloc client. Safe to re-run.

### 4. Verify

```bash
kubectl get pods -n <workspace>
```

Two pods in `Running` state: `mysql-...` and `tracebloc-jobs-manager-...`

Open [ai.tracebloc.io](https://ai.tracebloc.io) — your node should show **Online**.

<Tip>
  Stuck? Check the [Troubleshooting](/environment-setup/troubleshooting) page.
</Tip>
